Lulu Antariksa- Biography

Lulu Antariksa was born on 22 August 1995, under the sign of Leo, in Los Angeles County, California, USA, and is a 24-year-old actress best known for her roles as Stevie Baskara in “How To Rock,” Rowan Fricks in “T@gged,” and Penelope Park in “Legacies.”

What is the Net Worth of Lulu Antariksa? Salary, Earnings

Antariksa has a net worth of over $500,000, which she has amassed through her acting career since the early 2000s when she appeared in a number of TV shows and films. She’s also extended her fortune by serving as a panelist on a children’s game show and starring in various music videos.

Lulu Antariksa- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings

Lulu is the youngest of four children born to a German mother, Kara Burger Antariksa, and an unknown Indonesian father. Chris and Mark Antariksa are two of her three older brothers. Her nationality is American, and she is of mixed ethnicity.

Education Details

Lulu matriculated at Valencia High School in Placentia, California, when she was 15 years old. While in high school, she discovered a passion for not just performing but also modeling.
As a result, she appeared in school musicals such as “Mean Girls (The Musical)” and “Mad World.” She has studied singing and dance, as well as the piano, guitar, bass, ukulele, and saxophone.

Lulu Antariksa- Relationship, Boyfriend

Lulu Antariksa is still legally single and has no children. She was romantically involved with Maxwell George ‘Max’ Schneider, a notable singer-songwriter and actor, in 2013. They even worked together on a few tunes. Murphy, Lulu’s cat, is her pet.

Lulu Antariksa- Professional Career

Lulu made her television debut as Nina in the episode “The Masked Eagle: Part 1” of the PBS sitcom “American Family” in 2002, followed by guest-starring roles as Madeline in the ABC sitcom “According To Jim” episode “Slumber Party” in 2003, and Anna in the NBC medical drama series “ER” episode “Touch And Go” in 2004.

She went on to play Erica in the primetime series “Head Cases” (2005), a whispering toddler in the USA Network’s comedy-drama series “Monk” (2006), and young Anna in the NBC sci-fi series “Gemini Division” (2008), among other parts.

Ascend to Fame

Lulu Antariksa rose to prominence in 2012 with her breakout performance as bassist Stevie Baskara in David Israel and Jim O’Doherty’s live-action comedy series “How To Rock.”
The series follows the rivalry of two rock bands and is based on Meg Haston’s 2011 book “How To Rock Braces And Glasses.” It aired on Nickelodeon, a children’s cable channel, from February to December 2012, however it was discontinued after one season. Lulu appeared as a panelist in seven episodes of Nickelodeon’s panel game program for kids, “Figure It Out,” the same year. In 2012, she appeared in Kurt Hugo Schneider’s music videos “Lightning” and “Maroon 5 Medley,” as well as Daniel Durston’s music video “All Your Love.”

Success in the Future

Following cameo appearances on TV shows such as “Jessie” and “Kickin’ It,” Lulu joined the cast of the musical drama web series “Side Effects” from 2013 to 2014, playing the lead character Lexi Connolly. She later appeared as Kayla in an episode of The CW’s romantic musical comedy-drama series “Crazy Ex-Girlfriend,” and as Rowan Fricks in Hannah Macpherson’s psychological thriller web series “T@gged” (2016-2018), which aired on the streaming service go90.

Lulu Antariksa was cast as Anna in Josh Mendoza’s 2018 post-apocalyptic thriller “What Still Remains,” and then as Penelope Park in The CW’s fantasy drama series “Legacies” (2018-2019), opposite Aria Shahghasemi, Danielle Rose Russell, and Kaylee Bryant.

It was created by Julie Plec and is a spin-off of the fantasy supernatural drama series “The Originals,” which was itself a spin-off of the supernatural adolescent drama series “The Vampire Diaries.”

She was most recently seen as Silvia in Lisa J Dooley’s short horror film “The Lonely Host.” Furthermore, she will play Jen in the upcoming fantasy horror film “Witch Hunt,” alongside Christian Camargo, Elizabeth Mitchell, and Gideon Adlon — the film is now in post-production.
