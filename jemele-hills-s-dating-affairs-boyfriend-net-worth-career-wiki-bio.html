Jemele Hill Career

At the early stages of her career, Hill started out as the sports writer for the ‘Raleigh News & Observer.’ Later, she began writing for the ‘Detroit Free Press’ mainly focusing on the Michigan State football and basketball.

In November 2006, she got hired as a national columnist for the ESPN. She started hosting many ESPN programs including Outside the Lines, The Sports Reporters and ESPN First Take. During the 2008 NBA Playoffs, she got suspended because of referencing Adolf Hitler in an article about the Boston Celtics and the Detroit Pistons. In an editorial, she also described why she couldn’t support the Celtics. She wrote:

“Rooting for the Celtics is like saying Hitler was a victim. It’s like hoping Gorbachev would get to the blinking red button before Reagan.”

Hill was suspended for a week, but she later apologized for her actions through ESPN.

Jemele Hill Wiki-bio: Age, Ethnicity, Nationality and more

On 21st December 1975, Jemele Hill was born to Jerel Brickerson and Denise Dennard. Her full name is Jemele Juanita Hill but is commonly known as Jemele Hill.

She was born in Detroit, Michigan, USA. In 1980, she and her mother moved to Houston but later settled back to Detroit. She graduated from Mumford High School and for higher education, she attended the Michigan State University.

As of 2018, Hill is 42 years old. Her zodiac sign is Sagittarius. She follows Christianity and belongs to the black ethnicity. She has a slim body with a height of 5 feet 6 inches.
