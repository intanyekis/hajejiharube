Overall Meaning

In Kevin Gates's song "Satellites," he begins by stating that he is everyone's favorite and is often compared to the legendary Michael Jackson. The chorus of the song, "Satellites, never really made love, but she gon' get it right," implies that the woman he is with has not had much experience with intimacy, but he is willing to teach and guide her. The song also speaks about Gates's past and his current lifestyle as a drug dealer, but he is looking for a constant and devoted partner who will stay by his side through thick and thin. Throughout the song, he emphasizes that he is always on the grind and needs someone who can keep up with his lifestyle.


The chorus is repeated several times throughout the song, emphasizing that Gates and his partner are both learning and growing in their relationship. He wants to show her the ropes of love and intimacy, and although she may receive criticism from friends, he is committed to helping her understand what it truly means to be loved. Gates also implies that he has had his fair share of issues and struggles, but he is determined to overcome them and move forward. The song expresses his desire for a meaningful relationship and a partner who will always be faithful and true to him.


Overall, "Satellites" is a powerful and reflective song that speaks to Kevin Gates's journey and search for love and stability in his life. Through his lyrics, he shows vulnerability and strength, both in his personal life and as an artist. The song has resonated with many fans and has become one of Gates's most popular tracks.
