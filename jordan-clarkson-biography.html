Who is Jordan Clarkson?

Jordan Clarkson is a Filipino-American basketball player. He plays for Utah Jazz.

Prior, he played for the Cleveland Cavaliers of the National Basketball Association (NBA) along with Kevin Love, George Hill, and LeBron James. Also, he was a part of the team Los Angeles Lakers for four years.

Age, Parents, Siblings, Family, Ethnicity

Jordan Clarkson was born on June 7, 1992, in Tampa, Florida. He was born Jordan Taylor Clarkson. Jordan was born to his father Mike Clarkson, and mother Annette Davis. He has a brother named Bear Clarkson.

Jordan holds American and Philippine citizenship and his ethnicity is African American.

Education

Jordan Clarkson attended Karen Wagner High School in San Antonio and signed a National Letter of Intent to play college basketball at the University of Tulsa.

After that, he attended the University of Missouri from 2013 to 2014.

Jordan Clarkson: Professional Life, Career

Jordan Clarkson averaged 20 points, six rebounds, and four assists per game as well, which leads his team to the semi-final of the Class 5A championship during his junior year, and averaged 18.9 points per game in his senior year.

He averaged 18.9 points, 6.1 rebounds 3.4 assists, and 2.1 steals as a senior at the University of Tulsa.

Moreover, he was honored with First Team All-District 27-5A, First Team All-Region, and San Antonio Express-News Super Team in 2009-010.

He has been honored with the 2011 Conference USA All-Freshman team during his first season with Tulsa.

After that, Jordan Clarkson was selected to the All-Conference USA first team and the NABC All-District 11 team in his sophomore season. Jordan was moved to Missouri in May 2012 and named the 2014 All-SEC second team in his junior season.

He debuted with the Los Angeles Lakers in 2014 and was selected as the 46th overall pick in the 2014 NBA draft by the Washington Wizards. However, Jordan was traded to Los Angeles Lakers for $1.8 million.

Moreover, he played with the Angeles for four seasons, and during that time he also became Rookie of the Month (2015), NBA All-Rookie First Team, and Rising Stars Challenge participant (2016).

Awards, Nominations

Jordan Clarkson won three Southeastern Conference Player of the Week honors and was named to the Wooden Award Midseason Top 25 list in January 2014 as well.

Also, he was awarded the First-team All-State by the Texas Association of Basketball Coaches, San Antonio High School Player of the Year, McDonald’s All-America Finalist, WOAI-TV Player of the Year for the San Antonio area (2009), and Faith Seven Game MVP (2010).
