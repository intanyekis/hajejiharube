Robert Reich has achieved great success in America. He is a political pundit, author, lawyer, and lecturer. From 1993 until 1997, Reich held the position of 22nd Secretary of Labor for the United States. He has backed and participated in the Democratic Party.

Robert Reich has worked for presidents Gerald Ford and Jim Carter, where he achieved some measure of success. Later, he served in the administration of President Bill Clinton as secretary of labor. After that, he gained quite a bit of notoriety. Robert Reich is renowned for serving on Barack Obama’s economic transition board while he was president.

At the Goldman School of Public Policy, Robert Reich has been a chancellor’s professor of public policy since 2006. Prior to it, he had held other lecturing positions, including one at Harvard University. He is well known for his work as a political analyst on many shows. Reich has written 18 novels, which is a remarkable accomplishment.

Biography

In Scranton, Pennsylvania, on June 24, 1946, Robert Reich was born to Mildred and Edwin Reich. His father had a business selling clothes for ladies. Multiple epiphyseal dysplasia, sometimes known as Fairbank’s disease, is a bone condition that causes low stature among other symptoms. Reich was diagnosed with it as a youngster. His illness made him a target for bullies as a child. He frequently looked to bigger lads for protection, Michael Schwerner being one among them. Schwerner was one of the three civil rights activists assassinated in Mississippi in 1964 by the Ku Klux Klan. Later, Reich credited this incident with inspiring him to seek a career in public service.

Reich attended Cross River, New York’s John Jay High School. He attended Dartmouth College on a National Merit Scholarship, majoring in history, and graduated with honors in 1968. Later, he was awarded a Rhodes Scholarship to study economics, politics, and philosophy at Oxford’s University College. He first met Bill Clinton, another Rhodes Scholar, while he was a student there. He later received a J.D. from Yale Law School, where Bill Clinton, Hillary Rodham, Clarence Thomas, and Richard Blumenthal were among his classmates. Following graduation, he worked for Chief Judge Frank M. Coffin of the United States Court of Appeals for the First Circuit as a legal clerk.

Career

Robert Reich began his career as an attorney and worked first as a law clerk and then as an assistant for Robert Bork, who was the US Solicitor General at the time. He was chosen by Jimmy Carter to join the Federal Trade Commission in 1977. He gained favor in politics as a result. Robert relocated to the John F. Kennedy School of Government in 1980, where he taught as well as penned The Next American Frontier and The Work of Nations, among other books and essays.

Robert Reich began working for Bill Clinton in 1992. In 1993, after Clinton was elected president, he was appointed secretary of labor, a position he held until 1997. Before serving on the economic advisory transition board for Barack Obama, he later worked as a professor at a number of colleges. He is not only a published author of books but also a contributing editor for publications like The New Republic, The American Prospect, and The New York Times.

Robert Reich became well-known through his work as a political analyst on several shows. He has had positions with CNN Tonight, Hardball with Chris Matthews, Kudlow and Company on CNBC, and others.
