Dream And His YouTube Channel

On February 8, 2014, Dream started uploading videos to his YouTube channel. He enjoys popularity among his followers, as evidenced by his more than 30 million subscribers and more than 2.9 billion cumulative views.

Dream also has six additional YouTube accounts where he posts a variety of content concerning Minecraft. His other YouTube accounts include Dream XD, Dream Manhunt, Dream Team, Dream Shorts, Dream Tech, and Dream Music. He received the Streamy Award for Gaming from YouTube in both 2020 and 2021.

After 2019, Dream began consistently posting new content to his account. His earliest video, which is still available, features him purposefully playing Minecraft poorly in order to “trigger” people. As of August 2021, the video had over 17 million views.

Dream’s most popular and well-liked show is “Minecraft Manhunt,” and he also took part in competitions that helped generate about $3,400 for charitable causes. In 2021, Dream said that he had given The Trevor Project, a charity for LGBT kids, US$140,000 (US$90,000 from fan donations and US$50,000 from the Dream Team). Despite having a positive reputation, he has been linked to various cheating and fraud scandals, which has made him a contentious figure.

As a result of his success, the YouTuber has also been mentioned in a number of news stories. In January 2021, Business Insider Steven Asarch attributed Dream’s growth in 2019 and 2020 “to his understanding of the YouTube algorithm.”
