English author and former actress, Tessa Dahl rose to fame for her role Celia Perry in the American mystery film, Happy Mother's Day, Love George. She is also known for her first book, Working for love.

Tessa was born as Chantal Sophia Dahl on 11th April 1957, in Oxford, England, United Kingdom. She holds a British nationality and her ethnic background is mixed (British-American). Find out more in detail about Dahl's personal life.

Tessa Dahl's Current Relationship Status

The sixty-two-year-old, Dahl is currently single after the failed marriage life. She was married twice and has involved in many relationship affairs as well.

Formal Marriages

Dahl was first married to a businessman, James Kelly in 1980. Most of their friends and family relatives attended their wedding ceremony held at the "Heaton House Farm" venue in Rushton Spencer, Macclesfield.

After living for eight in a marital relationship, they filed for divorce, citing irreconcilable differences in 1988. The court further announced their divorce settlement in mid-1988. 

The duo moreover welcomed two adorable children, two sons, Clover and Luke Kelly.

After the separation, Dahl again tied the knot with another businessman, Patrick Donovan who is the son of an Australian lawyer, Francis Patrick Donovan in 1992. Their wedding was held at the "TYTO" venue in Ingham, Queensland, Australia.

The marriage, however, didn't last long and they divorced because of some misunderstanding in the same year. They also share a lovely son, Ned Donovan from their marriage.

Past Affairs

Tessa was romantically involved in a blissful relationship with an English actor, Julian Holloway. The couple started dating in 1991. 

Together, they welcomed a beautiful daughter, Sophie Dahl who is an author and former model. After the birth of their child, they split up and Sophie was in the care of Tessa.

After their separation, Dahl began to date an English actor, David Hemmings in 1975. Shortly they departed their relationship in mid-1975. Similarly, she also dated with Peter Sellers, Bryan Ferry, Brian de Palma, and Dai Llewellyn.

The Time Magazine named Dahl as one of the five most beautiful women in the world who was spared jail on the condition she was in treatment and therapy for alcoholism and drug addiction.
